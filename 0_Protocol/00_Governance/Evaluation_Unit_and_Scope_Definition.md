# Evaluation Unit and Scope Definition

Status: Canonical (Methodological Definition)  
Version: 1.0  
Applies to: QA Phase (S0, S1) and Outcome Evaluation  
Last Updated: 2025-12-17

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document formally defines the **unit of evaluation** and the **scope of assessment** in the present study.
Its purpose is to eliminate ambiguity regarding *what is being evaluated*, *at what granularity*, and *for which outcomes*, thereby ensuring methodological clarity, reproducibility, and interpretability of results.

---

## 2. Primary Evaluation Unit

### 2.1 Definition

The **primary unit of evaluation** in this study is the **individual Anki-style question–answer card**, defined as a self-contained learning item comprising:

- A clinical or conceptual prompt (question)
- A corresponding answer with explanatory rationale

Each card is treated as an independent educational artifact for the purposes of accuracy, quality, and efficiency assessment.

### 2.2 Rationale

The selection of Anki cards as the primary evaluation unit is motivated by the following considerations:

1. **Clear unit boundary**  
   Anki cards provide a well-defined, discrete unit, enabling item-level scoring and reproducible evaluation.

2. **Compatibility with quantitative metrics**  
   Accuracy, partial correctness, educational quality, and efficiency can all be operationalized at the card level.

3. **Alignment with examination-oriented learning**  
   Anki-style items closely reflect the format and cognitive demands of radiology board examinations and structured review.

4. **Reduction of interpretive ambiguity**  
   Unlike free-text reports or narrative explanations, Anki cards minimize ambiguity in defining correctness and relevance.

---

## 3. Secondary Educational Artifacts

### 3.1 Definition

**Infographics and other visual materials** generated by the pipeline are classified as **secondary educational artifacts**.

### 3.2 Role in the Study

Secondary artifacts:

- Serve as **supportive or illustrative materials**
- May enhance comprehension or engagement
- Are **not included in the primary quantitative evaluation**

They are reviewed qualitatively during QA for safety, clarity, and consistency, but they do not constitute units of statistical analysis.

### 3.3 Rationale for Exclusion from Primary Evaluation

Infographics are excluded from primary evaluation because:

- Their informational content often overlaps with text-based cards
- Visual interpretation introduces additional variability
- Objective, item-level accuracy scoring is less well-defined
- Repeatability across runs may be lower than for text-based artifacts

Accordingly, infographics are treated as **presentation-layer outputs**, not core evaluative units.

---

## 4. Scope of Quantitative Evaluation

Quantitative evaluation is conducted **exclusively at the Anki card level** and includes the following domains:

### 4.1 Technical Accuracy

- Correctness of the primary concept, diagnosis, or principle
- Appropriateness of reasoning and explanation

Accuracy is assessed using a **graded item-level scale** (defined in a separate metric specification document).

### 4.2 Educational Quality

Educational quality is evaluated independently from technical accuracy and reflects:

- Relevance to board-level or curriculum-defined core topics
- Appropriateness of content depth
- Contribution to efficient learning

Quality metrics are designed to capture educational validity rather than factual correctness alone.

### 4.3 Efficiency (Exploratory)

Efficiency-related outcomes (e.g., time to comprehension or editing time) are treated as **exploratory endpoints**, assessed only when measurement is feasible and reliable.

---

## 5. Scope of Qualitative Evaluation

Qualitative evaluation applies to:

- Explanatory clarity
- Risk of misleading emphasis
- Alignment with intended learner level

Qualitative findings are used to contextualize quantitative results and inform discussion, but are **not used as primary outcome measures**.

---

## 6. Implications for Subgroup and Longitudinal Analyses

Because the evaluation unit is fixed at the item level, subgroup analyses focus on:

- Aggregated card-level outcomes within individuals
- **Within-subject changes** relative to baseline
- Differences in response patterns across learner experience levels

Between-subject comparisons are interpreted cautiously, recognizing individual variability in baseline knowledge and study behavior.

---

## 7. Summary Statement

In summary, this study defines the **individual Anki-style question–answer card** as the sole primary unit of evaluation.
This decision enables precise, reproducible, and educationally meaningful assessment of LLM-generated learning content, while relegating visual materials to a supportive, non-evaluative role.

This definition underpins all subsequent QA procedures, metric definitions, and statistical analyses.
