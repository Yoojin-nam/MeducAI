## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines **exploratory efficiency-related outcome measures** for the evaluation of LLM-generated Anki cards.

Efficiency is treated as a **secondary, exploratory construct**, recognizing both its potential importance and the methodological challenges associated with precise measurement in educational settings.

The primary goal of including efficiency measures is to generate **hypothesis-generating evidence** rather than to support confirmatory statistical claims.

---

## 2. Conceptual Definition of Efficiency

In the context of this study, **efficiency** refers to the *time-related cost or benefit* associated with using an educational item to achieve understanding or readiness for examination.

Efficiency is conceptually distinct from:

- **Accuracy** (factual correctness)
- **Educational quality** (pedagogical value)

A card may be accurate and high-quality but inefficient if it requires excessive time or cognitive effort to extract the core message.

---

## 3. Scope and Limitations

### 3.1 Exploratory Nature

Efficiency measures:

- Are not used as primary or co-primary endpoints
- Are not required for every participant or every card
- Are reported descriptively and interpreted cautiously

No formal hypothesis testing is planned for efficiency outcomes.

### 3.2 Measurement Challenges

Efficiency is influenced by:

- Individual learner speed and baseline knowledge
- Familiarity with Anki or similar tools
- Topic complexity

Accordingly, efficiency metrics are designed to be **simple, minimally intrusive**, and optional.

---

## 4. Efficiency Measurement Options

One or more of the following approaches may be used, depending on feasibility.

### 4.1 Self-Reported Time to Comprehension

**Definition**  
Estimated time required for a learner to feel they understand the content of a card.

**Collection Method**  
- Single-item self-report (e.g., “Approximately how many seconds/minutes did it take to understand this card?”)

**Advantages**  
- Easy to collect
- Low participant burden

**Limitations**  
- Subjective
- Susceptible to recall bias

---

### 4.2 Expert Editing Time

**Definition**  
Time required for an expert reviewer to revise a card to a publishable or examination-ready state.

**Collection Method**  
- Timer-based measurement during QA revision

**Advantages**  
- Objective
- Directly relevant to content production efficiency

**Limitations**  
- Depends on editor experience
- Not learner-centered

---

### 4.3 Relative Efficiency Compared to Baseline Materials

**Definition**  
Perceived or measured time difference when studying the same topic using existing materials versus the LLM-generated card.

**Collection Method**  
- Within-subject comparison
- Self-reported relative estimate

**Advantages**  
- Contextualizes efficiency gains

**Limitations**  
- Indirect
- Dependent on baseline material quality

---

## 5. Data Handling and Analysis

- Efficiency data are summarized using descriptive statistics (median, IQR)
- Extreme values are examined but not excluded unless clearly erroneous
- Results are reported as **exploratory observations**, not definitive effects

Where appropriate, efficiency measures may be stratified by learner level or topic complexity.

---

## 6. Relationship to Other QA Metrics

Efficiency measures are interpreted **in conjunction with**:

- Technical accuracy
- Educational quality

Efficiency is never used to justify acceptance of inaccurate or misleading content.

---

## 7. Reporting and Interpretation

Efficiency findings are:

- Clearly labeled as exploratory
- Presented separately from primary QA outcomes
- Used to inform discussion and future study design

Claims regarding efficiency are explicitly framed as **preliminary**.

---

## 8. Summary Statement

This document specifies a cautious, exploratory approach to measuring efficiency in LLM-generated educational content. By explicitly acknowledging measurement limitations and restricting efficiency to a secondary role, the framework balances methodological rigor with practical insight, while avoiding overinterpretation of time-based metrics.
